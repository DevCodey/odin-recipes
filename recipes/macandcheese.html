Mac and Cheese

Indulge in the comforting flavors of Southern-style baked mac and cheese. This soulful dish captures the essence of African American cuisine, boasting a velvety, cheese-laden sauce that envelopes tender elbow macaroni. With each bite, you'll experience the perfect balance of creamy textures and rich, savory flavors. The combination of sharp cheddar, gooey mozzarella, and nostalgic American cheese creates a harmonious blend that will leave you craving more. Enhanced with hints of garlic, onion, and mustard powder, this mac and cheese recipe takes on a delightful depth of flavor. For those seeking a touch of warmth, a pinch of cayenne pepper adds a subtle, tantalizing kick. Baked to golden perfection, the dish emerges from the oven with a crispy breadcrumb topping, offering a satisfying contrast to the luscious, cheesy interior. Whether enjoyed as a comforting main course or as a side dish alongside your favorite Southern entrees, this Southern-style baked mac and cheese is sure to become a beloved addition to your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup shredded American cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper optional, for a hint of heat
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs optional, for a crunchy topping

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit (175 degrees Celsius). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish and set aside.
  2. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously for about 1-2 minutes until the mixture turns golden brown and forms a paste (roux).
  4. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking constantly, ensuring there are no lumps. Continue cooking and whisking until the mixture thickens, about 5 minutes.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and add the shredded cheddar, mozzarella, and American cheeses to the saucepan. Stir until the cheeses have melted and the sauce is smooth.
  6. Add the garlic powder, onion powder, mustard powder, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper to the cheese sauce. Adjust the seasonings to your taste preference.
  7. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and mix well, ensuring all the macaroni is coated.
  8. Pour the mac and cheese mixture into the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.
  9. If desired, sprinkle breadcrumbs over the top of the mac and cheese to create a crispy crust.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the edges are bubbly.
  11. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley.
  12. Serve the Southern-style baked mac and cheese while it's still warm and enjoy its creamy, cheesy goodness!
