Turkey Wings

Experience the mouthwatering allure of Southern turkey wings, a beloved gem of African American cuisine. These turkey wings are a true testament to the rich flavors and soulful traditions of the South. Slow-cooked to tender perfection, the succulent meat effortlessly pulls away from the bone, revealing its moist and juicy essence. As you take your first bite, you'll be greeted by a symphony of robust spices, including garlic, onion, paprika, thyme, and rosemary, harmonizing to create a tantalizing flavor profile that dances on your palate. Each wing is lovingly coated with a sticky glaze, boasting a delicate balance of sweetness and tanginess, courtesy of the savory barbecue sauce. The result is a delectable dish that marries the natural succulence of the turkey with the bold flavors of the South. Served hot and garnished with fresh herbs or sliced green onions, these Southern turkey wings are a culinary delight that captures the heart and soul of Southern American cooking. Prepare to savor every tender, flavorful bite and embark on a journey of taste that celebrates the time-honored traditions of African American cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 4 turkey wings
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of barbecue sauce

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, thyme, rosemary, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Rinse the turkey wings and pat them dry with paper towels.
  4. Rub the spice blend onto the turkey wings, making sure to coat them evenly.
  5. Heat the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the turkey wings and sear them for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
  6. Pour the chicken broth into the skillet and bring it to a simmer.
  7. Cover the skillet with a lid or aluminum foil and transfer it to the oven.
  8. Bake the turkey wings for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily falls off the bone.
  9. Remove the skillet from the oven and pour the barbecue sauce over the turkey wings, making sure to coat them evenly.
  10. Return the skillet to the oven and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until the barbecue sauce has caramelized and formed a sticky glaze.
  11. Serve the turkey wings hot, garnished with chopped fresh herbs or sliced green onions, alongside your favorite southern sides. Enjoy!
