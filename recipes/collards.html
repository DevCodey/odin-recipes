Collard Greens

Indulge in the captivating flavors and cultural heritage of collard greens, a quintessential dish in African American cuisine. A symphony of robust collard greens, slow-cooked with smoky bacon, succulent turkey, or flavorful ham hocks, beckons you to savor a taste of Southern tradition. As the pot simmers, the collard greens tenderize, absorbing the rich flavors of the accompanying meat, creating a harmonious balance of earthiness and savory notes. With each forkful, you are transported to a time when these humble greens nourished and nurtured generations, preserving culinary customs rooted in resilience and celebration. Whether enjoyed as a side dish or the star of the meal, this soulful combination of collard greens and meat invites you to experience the soul-soothing flavors that have become a cherished part of African American culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds collard greens, stems removed and leaves chopped
  • 6 slices of bacon, turkey bacon, or 2 smoked ham hocks
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a hint of heat)
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar (optional, for tanginess)

Recipe

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the bacon, turkey bacon, or ham hocks over medium heat until browned and crispy. If using ham hocks, allow them to render their flavorful fat.
  2. Remove the cooked meat from the pot and set it aside. If using bacon or turkey bacon, leave a small amount of fat in the pot for added flavor.
  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Saut√© until the onions become translucent and fragrant.
  4. Add the collard greens to the pot, stirring to combine them with the onion and garlic mixture. The greens will begin to wilt.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, smoked paprika, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir well to incorporate the seasonings.
  6. Return the cooked meat (bacon, turkey bacon, or ham hocks) to the pot, nestling it among the collard greens.
  7. Bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the collard greens cook for about 1 to 2 hours, or until they are tender and flavorful. Stir occasionally.
  8. If using ham hocks, remove them from the pot and shred the meat. Discard any bones, fat, or skin. Return the shredded meat to the pot.
  9. Adjust the seasoning to your taste preference. If desired, add apple cider vinegar for a tangy note.
  10. Simmer the collard greens for an additional 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  11. Serve the collard greens hot, along with their delicious meaty accompaniment. Pair them with cornbread, rice, or your preferred Southern side dishes for a truly satisfying meal.
