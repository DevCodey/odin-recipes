Turkey Wings

Southern turkey wings are a delicious and hearty dish that is perfect for a comforting meal. These turkey wings are typically slow-cooked with a blend of flavorful spices and seasonings, resulting in tender, juicy meat that falls right off the bone. This dish is popular in the southern United States and is often served with traditional sides like collard greens, mac and cheese, or cornbread.

Ingredients

  • 4 turkey wings
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of barbecue sauce

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, thyme, rosemary, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Rinse the turkey wings and pat them dry with paper towels.
  4. Rub the spice blend onto the turkey wings, making sure to coat them evenly.
  5. Heat the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the turkey wings and sear them for about 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
  6. Pour the chicken broth into the skillet and bring it to a simmer.
  7. Cover the skillet with a lid or aluminum foil and transfer it to the oven.
  8. Bake the turkey wings for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily falls off the bone.
  9. Remove the skillet from the oven and pour the barbecue sauce over the turkey wings, making sure to coat them evenly.
  10. Return the skillet to the oven and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until the barbecue sauce has caramelized and formed a sticky glaze.
  11. Serve the turkey wings hot, garnished with chopped fresh herbs or sliced green onions, alongside your favorite southern sides. Enjoy!
